# Plain-language summaries

Every preprint has a reader-oriented companion for people deciding what to read.
Each one translates the paper's practical question, why it matters, and the
capability or distinction it adds. These are not section-by-section abstracts
or ELI5 rewrites; they assume an experienced software engineer or systems
researcher who does not specialize in the paper's formal vocabulary.

These summaries are **explanatory companions**. A compact claim-boundary note
keeps the paper's level of warrant visible, but the overview is organized around
reader discovery. The companions are not canonical published artifacts, do not
silently revise a DOI-bearing paper, and introduce no new paper claims. The PDF
or manuscript remains the source to cite. When a paper is revised, its summary
should be reviewed in the same change.
