# Start Here

A short reader-ramp. Not the full theory — a customs checkpoint.

## The one idea

This corpus is about **admissibility**: when a system may treat a claim, observation, model output, receipt, or memory as **authority for action** — not merely as something that was said or observed.

> Monitoring asks *what happened*. Admissibility asks *whether what happened may be used*.

Three things sit under that question, and it helps to know which role each plays:

- **Δt / temporal coherence** is one *foundation* — the control-theoretic account of temporal mismatch and stale binding. It was the earliest spine of the program and remains a major thread, but it is not the whole animal.
- **Lean** is one *audit substrate* — selected prose claims are translated into formal objects to see which survive. It does not generate the theory; it stress-tests it.
- **Governed software** is the *downstream contact surface* — where the vocabulary gets forced against real operations. Contact, not authority.

## How to read the status of any claim

A higher paper number is **newer, not stronger**. Chronology is not dependency order and not a maturity ranking. To know what a specific claim is worth:

1. Check whether the paper is published (has a DOI) or still source-ahead in the [Corpus](corpus.md) table.
2. Check whether it was formalized in the [Formalization Index](formalization-index.md).
3. If formalized, check its verdict — **BROKEN / STALE / SOUND / OPEN / ADMITTED** — in the Lean [`CLAIM-REGISTER.md`](https://github.com/unpingable/lean/blob/main/CLAIM-REGISTER.md).

Claims that broke are kept on the record deliberately. See [Methodology](methodology.md) for why that is the point rather than an embarrassment.
