## The dual structure (chatty's hypothesis)

**Boundary calculus** = the formal apparatus.

Names the thing Lean is good at:

- admissibility predicates;
- authority boundaries;
- anti-laundering rules;
- what can and cannot derive;
- which transitions are licensed;
- how basis, standing, evidence, consequence, and time compose.

Shorthand: *the calculus of admissible transitions.*

**Admissibility cybernetics** = the domain / interpretive program.

Names the larger cybernetic claim:

- controllers can be locally correct while inadmissible;
- observability/controllability are not enough;
- feedback systems need authority conditions;
- control loops mutate obligation, witness, target, and time;
- "can steer" does not mean "may bind."

## The structural analogy

> boundary calculus : admissibility cybernetics :: differential geometry : general relativity

One is form apparatus; the other is the field of application / explanatory program. The analogy is not exact, but it captures the form/domain split that lets each side be cited independently.

## Why not collapse to one name yet

- **Boundary calculus alone** reads dry / formal / Lean-coded; undersells the cybernetic sweep.
- **Admissibility cybernetics alone** reads essayistic without the formal apparatus underneath; risks reading as policy/safety/ethics talk.
- Together they say: *a cybernetic theory of admissible control, with a formal boundary calculus underneath it.*

Collapsing early loses one of these readings. Let the dual structure sit until drafting tells us whether one is the program and the other is a component, or whether they really are a pair.

## Generator test

Over the next several drafting passes (P26 in flight; P27 still scaffold), ask:

1. Does this name make distinctions that were otherwise blurry?
2. Does it prevent misclassification between formal kernel work and cybernetic prose?
3. Does it help readers locate why admissibility is not merely ethics, policy, safety, or control theory?

The fourth question is load-bearing. A good program name **disciplines** the corpus; it does not give it a fake passport to annex everything. If the candidate starts being invoked to admit material that wouldn't otherwise be in scope, it's failing the test.

## Anti-recurrence note

This audit and the post-P27 reframe are filed here because the operator (and assistants) have circled this shape before — the "I want to find the next research program after this one" reflex that surfaces near the end of a sequence. You didn't accidentally do two at once; you finished one a while ago without noticing.
