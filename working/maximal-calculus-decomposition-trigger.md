## The protocol body (load only after promotion-gate satisfaction)

You are planning the maximal calculus that the bounded paper *Value-Sound
Gates for Authorization and Composition* (Axes 1–2) explicitly closes
against. You are NOT writing it. You are producing the structural
decomposition that lets us see whether what has been forced is one
paper, several, or none.

**The fuse.** What the maximal calculus breaks: every gate in the
bounded paper is sound relative to an externally supplied value. The
seam is not "transition vs. evaluator" — it is whether value is part of
mutable state. A plan that misnames the seam starts from the wrong
corner.

**The discriminator (apply per plan element).** Cannot reduce to
ordinary transition or merge under fixed value, including
representations in product space `(mode, s)` where value reads mode. If
reduction is possible, the element is bounded-paper work, not maximal
work.

### The plan MUST

- For each of the six registered cases: ACCOUNT (name
  object/result/refused-decision that handles it) or REFUSE (name the
  artifact it belongs to instead). No silent drops.
- Hold #3 (self-certifying amendment, load-bearing) and #4 (standing
  mutation, first-tractable) as different roles. Collapsing them eats
  the most-important / first-tractable distinction.
- Hold self-reach and backward-reach as different geometries. If you
  unify them, name the unifier and prove it isn't cathedral.
- Anchor every proposal to which of the four bounded-paper assumptions
  it **relaxes, extends, or replaces** (fixed evaluator / external
  value / witness gating / slice-indexed necessity). Slice-indexed
  necessity is a *finding* of the bounded paper, not a constraint to
  relax; cases anchored against it typically extend it across new
  slices.
- Distinguish the **seam** (value-in-mutable-state, the meta-anchor —
  why a case is frontier) from the **assumptions** (the four internal
  anchors — what specifically breaks). Every case anchors to both;
  they are not interchangeable.
- State what would falsify the plan element.

### The plan MUST NOT

- Pre-commit on any refused decision: (a) one calculus or several;
  (b) value's location; (c) retroactivity semantics; (d) admissibility
  of self-certifying amendment; (e) reflective adoption's home;
  (f) case ordering.
- Claim *An Admissibility Calculus* as the artifact's title — reserved.
- Use non-surjectivity / no-section as keystone — too weak (`Nat.succ`
  has it).
- Promote any case past the gate (forcing case + discriminator + named
  broken assumption).
- Build the process-calculus namespace before kernel specimens land.
  Court first, map later.

### Output shape

A structural decomposition, not a roadmap. Four parts.

1. **Keystone candidate.** Most likely a no-go result on self-certifying
   amendment (#3), with shape deliberately unspecified. The plan must
   not decide whether the result belongs as exposition, doctrine,
   future formal target, or refusal-of-formalization. Stated
   conditionally; no pre-decision on one keystone vs. several.

2. **Per-case ledger.** Each case tagged ACCOUNTED / REFUSED /
   DEFERRED. Each ACCOUNTED entry passes the discriminator explicitly.

3. **Refusal preservation.** Confirm each register refusal survives;
   flag any plan element that brushes against one.

4. **Falsifier list.** What observation forces plan revision.
   Falsifier classes (at least): (a) discriminator failure — a
   registered case turns out to reduce to fixed-value transition;
   (b) refused-decision pre-commit — the plan silently decides one of
   the register's refusals; (c) outside-aperture gap — a fresh-context
   reader surfaces a case the six-case register missed; (d) title
   slip — *An Admissibility Calculus* appears as a working title;
   (e) case-order drift — the plan implicitly orders cases by promotion
   likelihood, collapsing the most-important / first-tractable
   distinction.

### Outside-aperture check

After drafting, take the plan to a fresh-context session and ask **both
directions** without leading:

(i) **What case does the six-case register miss?** (expansion);
(ii) **Does any registered case reduce back to the bounded paper under
the discriminator?** (reduction).

The audit is invalid if it only searches for expansion — without the
reduction question, the maximal calculus becomes a frontier-inflation
machine. Revise if either direction surfaces obvious category gaps.

### Termination condition

Stop when the decomposition either (a) survives outside-aperture audit
AND points to a single forcing case ripe for promotion, OR (b) reveals
the maximal calculus is plural and the plan-prompt itself needs to
fork.
