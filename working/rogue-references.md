# Rogue reference notes: Nyquist and Ellul

These are not "influences" in the heroic-origin sense. They are useful triangulation points — coordinate axes, not ancestry.

## Nyquist / aliasing

Nyquist names the clean-room discipline: define the sampling regime correctly so the signal can be reconstructed without aliasing.

My recurring move is almost the operational inverse: start from the aliasing artifacts — stale dashboards, contradictory witnesses, green liveness with degraded coverage, cached answers mistaken for fresh authority, demos mistaken for theory — and infer what sampling/control boundary must have failed.

Compact version:

> Nyquist designs against aliasing. I read aliasing as testimony.

This is not a claim to "reverse Nyquist." It is a diagnostic posture: distortion is evidence of the regime that produced it.

The pairing:

- **Nyquist:** prevent epistemic corruption by respecting signal limits.
- **This work:** diagnose epistemic corruption by studying the artifacts left when signal limits were violated.

Keeper line:

> **The system tells the truth where it aliases.**

Not because the artifact is "true" directly, but because distortion reveals the hidden sampling regime, compression boundary or authority shortcut. The lie leaves fingerprints.

### Where the aliasing frame maps across the stack

- Δt collapse = temporal aliasing
- dashboard/KPI disease = managerial aliasing
- "working demo" as theory = evidentiary aliasing
- AI usage metrics as competence = technique aliasing
- stale basis binding mutation = authority aliasing
- cached answer mistaken for fresh truth = DNS aliasing, because of course

## Ellul / technique

Ashby gives the control-system leg: variety, regulation, constraint, requisite complexity.

Ellul gives the missing institutional leg: technique does not merely solve problems; it expands, standardizes, measures, justifies itself, and reorganizes human judgment around its own requirements.

That matters because many AI / SRE / dashboard failures are not just bad control loops. They are control loops absorbed into technique as an institutional imperative.

Compact version:

> Ashby explains why a regulator needs variety. Ellul explains why the regulator becomes mandatory, measurable, and eventually confused with virtue.

### The measurement inversion (Chris)

> "If you can measure it, you can manage it" inverted: if it cannot be measured, management replaces it with something measurable.

This is the Ellul move in miniature: measurement begins as an aid to judgment, then becomes the admission condition for what judgment is allowed to see.

Use Ellul as the rogue reference for the institutional dimension: it explains the social pressure that makes control surfaces harden into mandatory technique.

## The lane

These are not origin claims. They are coordinates.

> Nyquist helps name the signal failure; Ellul helps name why the failure becomes institutional technique.

Two failure modes to watch:

- **Over-lineage** — "my method combines Nyquist, Ellul, Ashby…" Résumé-core. Wrong.
- **Over-formalization** — "this constitutes an inverse Nyquistian epistemology…" Manifesto in a fake mustache. Wrong.

Modest but sharp: I diagnose systems by reading aliasing artifacts as evidence of failed sampling and control boundaries. Ellul names why those boundaries get reinforced as obligation.

Working note, not lineage claim.
