## The keeper: a three-way separation (already licensed by the economics literature)

> world event  ‚âÝ  resolution predicate  ‚âÝ  traded payoff

A prediction-market price is the price of a **contract-defined contingent payoff** ‚Äî
distorted by participant preferences, liquidity, and settlement risk ‚Äî **not necessarily
a physical probability of the world event** (Manski 2006; Wolfers & Zitzewitz 2006). The
laundering move is collapsing these three into one and selling the price back as
*knowledge* ("the market knows").

Three promotions to refuse, each an instance of machinery the corpus already owns:

- **market state ‚âÝ world state** ‚Äî the price witnesses a venue condition, not the event
  (signed‚âÝwitnessed / stored‚âÝtrue family).
- **resolved ‚âÝ true** ‚Äî settlement pays on an adjudicated input, not metaphysical truth.
  This is the **oracle problem** (Eskandari et al. 2021) + ordinary contingent-claim
  settlement semantics. Laundering magnitude scales with oracle discretion.
- **filled ‚âÝ settled** ‚Äî even the *market state* needs a witness if the venue can display
  one artifact and settle another (execution-layer floor; ruins the charitable "fine,
  prices only witness prices" fallback).

## What is legitimately new ‚Äî and it's prose, not Lean

The **selection argument**: authority-claiming anti-correlates with defensibility.
Crisp, short-horizon contracts (the closing-price feed) are the most honest as weak
instruments and draw the *least* "the market knows" energy because they're dull. The
contracts that accrue the most epistemic authority are exactly the ones high on horizon
and/or oracle-discretion ‚Äî vague predicate plus long horizon ("recession by EOY", "will X
be indicted"). Engagement selects for ambiguity; ambiguity is where the claim‚Üíwitness gap
is widest; the widest gap is then advertised as the *strongest* signal. **The vig is
heaviest on the priciest robes.** This gives the type-error a *motive* ‚Äî which is what
turns an observation into an indictment. Substack / Neutral Ambassador register, NOT a
kernel.

Two-axis (really three-axis) map of where the laundering lives:

| Axis | Question | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| freshness / horizon | how far is current stake from the future fact? | current money ‚Üí future knowledge |
| resolution discretion | how much judgment decides settlement? | governance decision ‚Üí truth |
| execution witness | is the displayed market state itself trustworthy? | venue artifact ‚Üí settled fact |

Table-flip zone = at least two axes high **and** the product copy says "the market knows."
The boring corner (low horizon, low discretion, witnessed execution) is a defensible weak
instrument; leave it alone.
