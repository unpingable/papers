# P25 abductive-intractability citation stack — primary and fallback

**Status:** drafting decision (resolved 2026-05-03). Use as the citation recipe for P25.
*Migrated from Claude memory on 2026-06-22 (`project-p25-rich-vanrooij-citation`).*

For P25's formal forcing-pressure source (intractability of abductive search →
substitution pressure → proxy installation → border collapse), the citation question
resolved 2026-05-03 in favor of routing around the cogsci-disciplinary literature.

**Primary stack (default for P25 drafting):**

1. **Bylander, Allemang, Tanner, Josephson 1991** — "The computational complexity of
   abduction." *Artificial Intelligence*. The anchor: frames abduction as finding the
   best explanation for data; complexity results show this is generally NP-hard /
   intractable.
2. **Selman & Levesque 1990** — AAAI proceedings, pp. 343–348. Abductive/default
   reasoning computational core. Useful if you want the lineage.
3. **Eiter & Gottlob 1995** — Logic-based abduction complexity; situates itself relative
   to Bylander et al.

**Why route around:** older formal substrate is structurally harder to deflect than
recent disciplinary discourse — dusty, unsexy, less deniable. Avoids importing the
AI-in-cogsci social fight unless P25 specifically needs it.

**How to apply (P25 paragraph shape):**

> Prior work in AI and computational complexity shows that abductive explanation is
> generally intractable under standard formulations [Bylander et al. 1991; Selman &
> Levesque 1990; Eiter & Gottlob 1995]. P25 treats this not as a claim that explanation
> is impossible, but as a forcing pressure: when the target relation cannot be tractably
> recovered, systems tend toward constrained proxies, heuristics, or substitute
> representations. The admissibility failure begins when that substitution is not
> declared.

**Fallback: Rich, de Haan, Wareham, van Rooij 2021** — "How hard is cognitive science?"
CogSci 2021, pp. 3034–3040. eScholarship: `escholarship.org/uc/item/8cr8x1c4`. Cite
*only* if P25 specifically needs the framing "cognitive science itself can be modeled as
abductive inference from behavior to hidden cognitive process." That framing imports the
disciplinary/AI-hype social context — van Rooij has a sustained public line on this —
which the older stack avoids. If used, fence narrowly: "for the complexity result; result
applies regardless of inference substrate."

**What NOT to cite either source for:** the slogan-tier "automated discovery is
accelerated confabulation." That is downstream interpretation, not a result either paper
supplies. Keep the actual mechanism chain (intractability → substitution pressure → proxy
installation → border collapse) in the prose; leave the slogan in the gift shop.
