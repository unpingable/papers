# Admissibility doctrine stubs — declared substitution

**Status:** candidate handles, NOT ratified doctrine. Name-early discipline.
*Migrated from Claude memory on 2026-06-22 (`project-admissibility-doctrine-stubs`).*

Two doctrine stubs surfaced 2026-05-03 during P25 citation-stack triage. Recorded per
name-early discipline; not yet ratified, but repeated enough across the admissibility
family to deserve a handle.

**Stub 1 (general admissibility-family form, prescriptive):**
> Heuristic admissibility requires declared substitution.

**Stub 2 (P25-native sharper form, prescriptive):**
> A tractable proxy must declare the target relation it does not recover.

**Stub 3 (descriptive twin of stub 2, names the failure mode):**
> The tractable thing will always try to wear the name of the true thing.

**Stub 4 (P25-coldest form, prescriptive, post-2026-05-03 review):**
> A regulable proxy must not be permitted to inherit the name of an unobserved target.

Stub 4 is sharper than stub 2 for P25 specifically because it names *regulability* and
*unobserved target* — the two operative terms in P25's observability-asymmetry theorem.
Stub 2 is the looser admissibility-family form; stub 4 is the P25-native form. Stub 3 is
the descriptive twin. All three likely belong together in P25.

**Before/after reframe (added 2026-05-03):**
- *Before the intractability layer:* systems substitute V′ for V because V is
  unavailable, expensive, institutionally inconvenient, or politically awkward. (The
  "lazy/captured" failure family.)
- *After the intractability layer:* sometimes systems substitute V′ for V because
  recovering V from available observations is *formally intractable*. (The
  "computationally hostile target" failure family.)
- The danger in both cases is identical: pretending V′ is still V. But the
  *countermeasures* differ — the lazy/captured form responds to incentive redesign and
  disclosure rules; the formally-hostile form responds to declared-substitution
  discipline and explicit target-relation tagging, because there is no incentive fix for
  an undecidable problem.
- This is the practical content of the "missing implementation vs formally hostile
  target" distinction — these are not just two flavors of the same failure; they take
  different remedies.

**Why:** Both fall out of the formal chain *intractability → substitution pressure →
proxy installation → border collapse*. The mechanism repeats across:
- P25 substitution-refusal architecture (V′ standing in for V)
- governor doctrine (heuristic decisions need standing/scope/evidence/contestability)
- continuity doctrine (reliance on proxy without declared revalidation condition)
- NQ shape (output observed ≠ mechanism known; receipt present ≠ authority valid)
- proposed "missing implementation vs formally hostile target" distinction

The recurrence is what makes this naming earn its keep — naming-after-the-fact would
have asymptotic cost across the constellation.

**How to apply:**
- Candidate primitives for the admissibility-family paper if it gets written, OR for P25
  directly if drafting hits the section that needs it.
- NOT yet ratified as project doctrine; treat as candidate handles.
- Sharper version (Stub 2) probably belongs to P25; general version (Stub 1) probably
  belongs to the admissibility-family paper.
- Distinguish from the slogan-tier "automated discovery is accelerated confabulation" —
  these stubs name the mechanism; that line is gift-shop merchandise.

**Adjacent unresolved primitive:** "missing implementation vs formally hostile target"
as a distinction the corpus may need to make crisply. Doesn't yet have a doctrine-stub
form. Worth watching for the third occurrence.
