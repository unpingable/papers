# OutOfScope: no silent conversion of moss into evidence

**Status:** UNRATIFIED jurisdictional rule. Calibration for the
apparatus's own appetite.
**Date filed:** 2026-06-09.
**Custody:** `working/tooltheory/` — meta-methodological. The rule
governs the apparatus; it is not part of the apparatus.

## The rule

The kernel apparatus has **no jurisdiction** over work that does not
make a claim seeking to bind, authorize, justify, convert, or
constrain.

- Garden / play / terrarium / shitpost / pre-coffee render / Hermes
  / weird small fungal metaphor work: **outOfScope by construction.**
- It may be observed, enjoyed, abandoned, resumed, renamed, allowed
  to rot, allowed to grow, allowed to be moss.
- It may **NOT** be cited as evidence for the courthouse.
- It may **NOT** be promoted into theorem substrate by retrospective
  closure.
- It may **NOT** be asked what it proves.
- **It proves nothing. That is its job.**

## The diagnostic question

Before the apparatus touches any artifact:

> Does this artifact make a claim that seeks to bind, authorize,
> justify, convert, or constrain?

- **No** → kernel has no jurisdiction. Move along. Do not file. Do
  not adjudicate. Do not annex as precursor evidence.
- **Yes** → ordinary kernel discipline applies (receipts, bridges,
  cuts, refusals, custody class).

Examples by classification (illustrative, not exhaustive):
- Terrarium / emergence / observation work that asserts nothing →
  **no jurisdiction.**
- Pre-coffee Math Fairy / Hermes-shaped output / garden shitposts →
  **no jurisdiction** unless the fairy starts issuing writs.
- Lean theorem statement → **yes, full jurisdiction.**
- Receipt kernel / refusal artifact → **yes.**
- Admissibility doctrine claim → **catastrophically yes.**

## The capture mechanism this defends against

The failure mode is not theoretical capture by a false framework.
The framework is largely correct. The failure mode is **annexation
by retrospective closure** — non-claim-making work, especially
unfinished emergence work, getting silently reinterpreted as
"precursor evidence" for the later apparatus.

The pull comes from the **verification-loop reward gradient**:

- Adjudicative / kernel / claim-routing work has crisp completion
  events (theorem green, receipt resolved, bridge gated). The
  verifiers (Lean, grep, CI, collaborator models) confirm closure.
- Garden / emergence / observation work has no such termination.
  Outputs are "huh, watch that" — no green, no receipt, no fairy.
- Run any metabolism through that reward gradient long enough and
  the portfolio drifts courthouse-ward. The lab partners are
  verifiers; the work migrates toward what they can verify.

The defense is not "make more time for play" (HR-approved wallpaper
paste). The defense is **jurisdictional**: garden work is outside
the apparatus's scope by construction, and the apparatus does not
get to file it, cite it, validate it, or retrospectively annex it
as evidence for its own framework, ever.

## What this protects

- Non-claim-making work from being silently load-bearing-ified.
- The apparatus from over-extending itself into a wellness-
  administration framework wearing the apparatus's clothes.
- The author from confusing "everything must produce a receipt" with
  "everything should be done." Receipts belong at claim boundaries.
- The kernels' own honesty: a framework whose strongest move is
  refusing improper conversion would be embarrassed to perform
  improper conversion on its own pre-history.

## What this does NOT mean

- Not "garden work is therefore special / sacred / protected as a
  rest activity." The rule is jurisdictional, not therapeutic. The
  apparatus has nothing to say about it because it makes no claim;
  whether and how to do it is outside the apparatus's competence
  either way.
- Not "the apparatus is bad and should feel bad." The kernel work is
  load-bearing for collaborator-model-mediated work — confabulated
  theorem names are real, fake load-bearing beams are real, and the
  custody discipline is the appropriate immune response. The capture
  failure mode is *over-extension*, not the apparatus's existence.
- Not a license for the apparatus to police whether something is
  "real play" or "still working." The OutOfScope verdict means
  silence, not approval and not disapproval. Audible silence is the
  signal.
- Not a permanent ruling. An artifact that **becomes** claim-making
  later (e.g., a garden observation that the author wants to assert
  as a finding about cognition) acquires jurisdiction at that point
  — at which point the conversion is **explicit**, not silent, and
  ordinary custody applies. The rule blocks silent conversion, not
  conversion as such.

## The keeper line

> **No silent conversion of moss into evidence.**
>
> The courthouse may remain armed. The woods are not a filing
> cabinet.

## Implementation (what changes)

Nothing in the Lean tree. Nothing in the kernel modules. Nothing in
any apparatus file.

The change is **author-side**: when about to file, adjudicate,
audit, or cite something, run the diagnostic question first. If the
answer is "no claim," close the apparatus and leave the artifact
alone. The rule self-enforces by not being executed.

If the apparatus catches itself trying to annex non-claim-making
work as evidence anyway — *that's* the receipt-shaped noise the
receipt-integrity rule warns about, applied to the framework's
appetite rather than to individual theorem names.
