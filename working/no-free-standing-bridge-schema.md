## The umbrella

> **A bridge that pretends to be free collapses the gate it crosses.**

## Math lineage & prior art (anticipatory; relayed, VERIFY before any paper)

This is not a new branch of math under the couch. It is a recognizable
**local-to-global obstruction** pattern — *absence of a global section / failure of
descent (gluing) of compatible local data* — wearing governance boots. Recording the
lineage so review doesn't surprise us with "this is just sheaf cohomology," and so the
contribution is stated honestly (the *instantiation* + the *bridge-price-as-feature*
framing, NOT a claim to have proved sheaf/obstruction theorems).

Per-axis math analogue (the resemblance, not an identity):

| Axis | Local-to-global analogue |
|---|---|
| Temporal | local step sections fail to glue into a trajectory section |
| Observer | consumer-indexed verdicts fail to collapse into a global section |
| Deadlock | locally valid transitions fail to compose into a global transition |
| Custody | plural local observations fail to form a singular authority certificate |
| Reflexive | internal operation fails to lift to meta-rule authority |

- **Sheaves / global sections** — the closest family. "Local section ⇏ global section
  unless compatibility/descent data exists" is our umbrella almost verbatim. The observer
  theorem is the literal match — `HasGlobalSection force ↔ ConsumersAgree force` is
  global-section language naked (robe now applied).
- **Indexed / fibered categories (Grothendieck construction)** — `Force : Consumer →
  Artifact → Verdict` is verdicts living in fibers indexed by consumers; a "force stamp on
  the artifact" tries to erase the base index. The category error, dryly: *it mistakes an
  indexed judgment for an absolute one* (`verdict in fiber c ⇏ verdict independent of c`).
- **Obstruction theory** — the bridge-price family is obstruction-shaped: not just "local
  data doesn't globalize," but "asserting a free bridge collapses the distinction the
  boundary existed to preserve." Mood, not cohomology: *the obstruction is not a bug; it is
  the boundary doing its job.*
- **BFT quorum intersection** — the custody row is standard quorum-intersection safety
  (`n=3f+1`, `2f+1` quorums overlap in `≥f+1`, ≥1 honest). Load-bearing caveat preserved:
  intersection is not consensus; it is safety only with the honest-no-double-certify lock.
- **Program-logic compositionality** — "property holds locally ⇏ preserved by
  composition" is the rely/guarantee, separation-logic, ownership-type warning. Ours is the
  governance-flavored version: *if composition needs a rule, pretending it is automatic is
  unsound.*

**Name candidates.** Math-respectable: *boundary-indexed local-to-global obstruction*.
User-facing (keep it): *No Free Standing Bridge*. The math-adjacent money sentence: *a
local judgment indexed by a context does not determine a global judgment unless compatible

cravat.

  failure = `no_conflicting_quorum_certificates`. *Not yet a global-section encoding.*
