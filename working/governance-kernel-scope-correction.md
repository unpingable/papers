# Governance kernel scope: anti-laundering engine, not legitimacy engine

**Filed:** 2026-06-15. **Status:** doctrine note — **FROZEN scope correction after adversarial
review.** The scope statement and the FENCE are load-bearing. The formal notes are guidance
for *future* kernels. The candidate theorem surfaces are **name-early, UNRATIFIED — not
authorized to build** until a forcing case earns each one.

## The correction (freeze this)

> **The kernel is not a legitimacy engine. It is an anti-laundering engine for declared
> orders.**

It does **not** abolish arbitrariness and it does **not** restrain the root. It prevents
emitters from converting **signatures, stances, classifications, contexts, narratives, or
local satisfactions** into higher-layer authority without a licensed crossing.

The root-abuse result — the part that keeps getting over-claimed away:

> If the root launders, the kernel cannot cure that. It can only force the laundering to
> **appear as root-level action, signed by the named root**, rather than hiding inside
> emitter-level evidence.

Bumper sticker: **it cannot abolish arbitrary power; it can force arbitrary power to sign
its own name.** The disease is incurable; the art is *where you let it live*. Bad systems
diffuse authority until every seam can launder. Governed systems force unlicensedness to
**pool at named roots**, where blame, audit, refusal, and succession can attach.

## THE FENCE (do not remove)

1. **Root trace does not imply legitimacy.** A clean chain back to the named root is not a
   proof the root was right — only that the root is *who acted*. Auditability ≠ correctness.
2. **Root abuse is visible, not cured.** Do not let any theorem or prose imply the kernel
   makes power legitimate. It makes power *attributable*.
3. **No legitimacy-engine reading.** If a downstream artifact starts claiming the kernel
   confers legitimacy / restrains the root / abolishes arbitrariness, that is scope creep
   back into the laundering the kernel refuses. Refuse it.

Composes with [[no-free-standing-bridge-schema]] (the lift schema is the *mechanism*; this
note is the *purpose*) and with [[anti-laundering-doctrine-map]] (the conversion-witness
binding rule is how a licensed crossing is typed).

## Corrective formal notes (for future kernels — not a build order)

Surfaced by adversarial review of a draft that garbage-bagged everything into one
`Emission` type. The type surgery, when a kernel is next built:

- **Do not use `Emission` as a garbage-bag type.** It collapses distinctions the kernel
  exists to keep apart.
- **Split the vocabulary:** `ActToken`, `Claim`, `ClaimKind`, `Defect`, `Pattern`.
- **Synchronic vs diachronic is a real seam, not a style choice:**
  - **Token-local admissibility** (does *this* `ActToken` / `Claim` discharge its kind
    here-and-now?) belongs in the **synchronic kernel**.
  - **Forum-shopping and laundering-over-time are diachronic `Pattern`s over sequences**,
    not token-local emissions. They belong in the **Δt / trajectory detector layer**.
  - This is the static/dynamic kernel split already named in [[bridge-obligation-lattice]];
    do not formalize a diachronic pattern inside a synchronic token check, and do not let a
    synchronic pass claim diachronic cleanliness.
- **`JurisdictionOf` is not magic.** It is a **trust basin / assignment authority** and
  must be *named, contestable, or refusable* — never an oracle that silently confers
  standing. A contested jurisdiction must be able to refuse token-local discharge.

## Candidate theorem surfaces (UNRATIFIED — name-early, do not build yet)

Each is a refusal, in the corpus's denote-and-refute style (the bad state is representable;
the claim is refused). Filed so they stop getting rediscovered; **each needs its own
forcing case before any Lean.** Classify a new one against [[anti-laundering-doctrine-map]]'s
family table before filing a sibling.

1. **self-classification does not discharge classification authority** — an emitter naming
   its own `ClaimKind` does not make the classification authoritative.
2. **stance does not discharge provenance** — a declared position is not a record of origin.
3. **standing does not witness cause** — having standing to speak ≠ evidence of what
   happened. (Adjacent to the custodian-binding refusal: *observed/instrumented ⇏
   accountable*.)
4. **root trace does not imply legitimacy** — (the FENCE item, as a theorem surface).
5. **contested jurisdiction refuses token-local discharge** — if `JurisdictionOf` is
   contested, the synchronic kernel must refuse, not silently pick a basin.
6. **synchronic admissibility does not imply diachronic cleanliness** — every token passing
   its local check does not clear the sequence of a laundering `Pattern`.
7. **locally licensed crossings can form a laundering pattern over time** — the diachronic
   dual of (6): each crossing individually licensed, the *trajectory* still a forum-shop /
   launder. This is the strongest one and the reason the Δt detector layer cannot be folded
   into the synchronic kernel.

Surfaces (1)–(3) are synchronic / token-local. (5) is synchronic with a jurisdiction
dependency. (6)–(7) are diachronic and belong to the trajectory layer — they are the formal
reason the two layers must stay separate.
