## Core framing

Apply Paper 25's control-substitution machinery (observability asymmetry → structurally forced proxy regulation) to elite / prestige discourse, where the regulated quantity is not truth or object-resolution but **admissibility**, **prestige coherence**, and **frame ownership**. The controller claims to regulate rigor / clarity / truth-tracking; it actually regulates visible rigor / visible sloppiness / admissibility markers; the substitution is invisible to the controller and to participants who cannot see the control law.

The key question is not whether the underlying mechanism is new — it is not. The key question is whether this domain introduces (a) a distinct **taxonomy of control moves** with its own explanatory weight, and/or (b) a distinct **observer-level signature** of substitution that Paper 25 does not name.

## What is inherited from Paper 25 (not new here)

1. **Structural unobservability of the real object.** The system cannot directly sense actual clarity / object-resolution; it gets prestige-proxy outputs (abstraction altitude, citation posture, definitional-policing rate, epistemic-hygiene signaling).
2. **Proxy regulation on the wrong channel.** The controller's cost function is written on "real clarity" (rhetoric stays fixed on $V$); its available observables load on prestige-proxy ($V'$); the substitution is forced by the filter geometry. This is Paper 25's $q_C = 0$ axiom applied to a new state space.
3. **Truth drift under stable surface order.** The surface can become arbitrarily well-regulated while the underlying object remains unresolved. $T_{rms}$ blows up while the controller's subjective report stays on "I am regulating truth." This is Paper 25's headline result in a new domain.

These are Paper 25 machinery in a new domain, not new theorems. Calling the control moves "rename / elevate / classify / moralize" gives the controller a more domain-specific actuator inventory; it does not mint a new substitution mechanism. Paper 25's Gramian argument runs through without modification — the T-axis of this larger state space lives near $\ker(O_T)$ under the same observability asymmetry that forces substitution in Paper 25's two-latent sim.

## Potentially novel object A: admissibility-move taxonomy

The discourse-specific control grammar, mapped to control-theoretic operations:

- **Renaming the thing** ($r_t$) → coordinate transform / reparameterization. Changes state-space basis so the controller operates in home-field coordinates; legitimate-looking, mechanically identical to changing variables.
- **Elevating the frame** ($e_t$) → gain increase / altitude shift. Moves to a representation where lower-order objections look naive. Functionally a controller gain boost coupled with observer-asymmetry manipulation.
- **Classifying disagreement as confusion** ($k_t$) → rejection filter. Converts substantive error into classification error. Efficient precisely because it avoids engaging the plant.
- **Invoking epistemic virtue / hygiene norms** ($v_t$) → internal-model-principle enforcement of the prestige landscape. The controller embeds an internal model of *high-status discourse generators*; disturbances not matching the model are asymptotically rejected.

**Test for whether the taxonomy does distinct work:** does it let a reader identify specific discourse moves in the wild more effectively than the Paper 25 theorem alone? Bar: a taxonomic entry that only says "this move is an instance of Paper 25 substitution" is not doing distinct work. An entry that names a specific mechanical signature (coordinate change vs gain boost vs rejection filter) and *predicts* when that move will fire (under what disturbance class, with what latency, to what observable effect) is doing distinct work.

**Candidate distinct-work example: the high-pass / derivative-kick prediction.** Blunt objections to prestige discourse arrive as high-frequency disturbances. The controller cannot respond with integral action (slow frame-tightening) fast enough; it must feedforward-kick with immediate abstraction to re-establish frame ownership before error propagates. Empirically this means: *the most precise jargon often appears immediately after the most blunt objection*. This is a prediction the taxonomy generates that Paper 25's general substitution theorem does not.

## Potentially novel object B: observer-fatigue as meta-observability signature

Paper 25 describes substitution from the controller's perspective — what the system does, how its cost function misaligns with its sensors. This note adds a third-party claim: what it feels like when an observer can perceive both the discourse output $y_t$ and the control effort $u_t$ shaping it.

> When an observer can perceive both the discourse output and the control effort shaping it, content begins to appear as governance metadata, and the result is fatigue rather than opacity.

Working formulation: observer fatigue occurs when a participant can track both (a) the presented content, and (b) the control law / admissibility machinery acting on that content. The result is a qualitatively different subjective signature from ordinary opacity. Real opacity says "I can't see the object." This says "I can see the object, the control law, and the prestige function all at once." That perceptual compression produces a fatigue function roughly of shape

$$T_t \;\sim\; \omega_1 p_t + \omega_2 |u_t| - \omega_3 C_t$$

— fatigue rises with visible rigor-signaling and boundary-control effort, and falls only when those efforts actually produce clarity gains. When $C_t$ is flat and $p_t, |u_t|$ are high, fatigue accumulates monotonically.

**Why this may be structurally distinct from Paper 25.** Paper 25 is a controller-side theorem: under observability asymmetry, substitution is forced. This is an *observer-side* claim: under observer ability to perceive the control law, the discourse becomes parseable as governance rather than as content. These can in principle come apart. A system can substitute without any observer perceiving the control law (covert substitution). An observer can perceive a loud control law that is not producing much substitution. So observer-legibility-of-the-control-law is formally separable from substitution itself, and constitutes a potential independent claim.

**Test for whether this is really distinct:** can it be stated as a proposition with its own formal content — e.g., as a statement about innovation-covariance divergence in a parallel Kalman filter the observer runs on the correct state-transition model? If yes (even informally), this is its own object. If the fatigue claim collapses to "observing substitution is annoying," it's Paper 25 with a subjective gloss, and belongs in §N of Paper 25 as prose ballast rather than standing separately.

## Relation to other series objects

- **Paper 23 (§3.3 case ii).** Inherits the same observability-null-space geometry Paper 25 inherits. No new relation.
- **Paper 24 (aggregation + witness-filter).** The "open witness inclusion" fix analogizes to prestige-discourse admitting low-status-but-correct objections as legitimate witnesses. The sibling argument from Paper 25 (aggregation improves SNR, does not rotate the observability subspace) tells us that *more participants* does not fix admissibility-control if their measurement maps are homogeneous with the house dialect. Heterogeneous-witness extension (Paper 24/25 follow-on) lives here too — what does it take for a blunt operator or domain expert to rotate the effective observability subspace of the system, rather than being absorbed as sensor noise?
- **Governor / admissibility doctrine.** The admissibility framework is a direct instantiation. Governor's contribution would be *separate sensor channels* for real clarity versus prestige-proxy, with receipt-lineage refusing the silent collapse that produces substitution. The connection is architectural, not decorative.
- **Latent Capitalism.** Strong fit. Prestige-discourse enclosure operates as admissibility-control over which concerns / critiques can even be formulated in acceptable register. The objective-aliasing mechanism is one way enclosure sustains itself against scrutiny — make the scrutiny itself read as register violation, so the regulator switches from regulating enclosure to regulating the discourse about enclosure. The "policy disguised as epistemology" line is Latent-Capitalism-native.

## Non-claims / guardrails

This note does **not** claim:

- a new underlying substitution mechanism (that is Paper 25);
- a new theorem independent of Paper 25's machinery;
- that every prestige discourse is reducible to this model — the falsification-guardrail applies, and the test questions ("would fixing observability change the outcome? is the degraded state now the regime?") must pass per instance.

Newness must live in either (1) the discourse-move taxonomy doing explanatory work beyond restating Paper 25 in domain language, or (2) the observer-fatigue result admitting a formulation distinct from Paper 25's controller-side claim. If both collapse, fold into Paper 25 as §N or Latent Capitalism chapter material; do not instantiate as a separate paper.

## Decision rule after Paper 25 lands

Standalone artifact is earned only if at least one of the following holds:

1. **The taxonomy is independently useful.** It produces predictions (like the high-pass / derivative-kick response to blunt objection) that Paper 25's theorem does not generate directly, and a reader can apply it as a portable interpretive framework.
2. **The observer-fatigue result is formally distinct.** It can be stated as a proposition about the meta-system (controller + observer) that is separable from Paper 25's controller-side substitution claim.
3. **The combined object supports a different audience / argumentative burden.** Paper 25 is aimed at readers who care about the control theorem; this would be aimed at readers who experience prestige-discourse fatigue and need the mechanism named. Different audience may warrant different artifact even if the formal content overlaps.

Non-exclusive: more than one of these can hold, and the object can legitimately become §N of Paper 25 *and* a Latent Capitalism chapter *and* a Substack essay simultaneously — the failure mode is paper inflation (three sovereign artifacts claiming independent necessity for what is one extension), not parallel deployment of the same extension to different audiences.

## One-line summary

Newness lives at the level of **move grammar** and **observer signature**, not underlying substitution dynamics.
