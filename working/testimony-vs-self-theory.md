# Testimony vs. Self-Theory

**Status:** Candidate doctrine. Not ratified. Forcing case (Carchidi essay reading, 2026-05-10) sufficient for naming; generator test not yet run. Filed per name-early discipline.

## Keeper

**Admitting testimony does not admit the testimony's preferred theory of itself.**

Operational form (imperative):

> **Admit the signal. Do not let the signal appoint itself system diagram.**

## Diagnostic

A witness may be reliable about its own state while unreliable about the substrate that produced it.

Examples:

- "I feel pain" ≠ "I know the cause."
- "I experience choosing" ≠ "choice is metaphysically uncaused."
- "The system reports healthy" ≠ "the system is covering the truth-space."
- "The model reports confidence" ≠ "the model has warrant."

The cut is between *what the testimony can warrant* and *what the testimony prefers to warrant*. Admissible testimony does not become more powerful because the witness has a theory of itself.

## Laundering chain

Recurring failure mode:

> methodological limitation → evidentiary exclusion → effective ontology

Less polite: *"we cannot inspect X directly" becomes "X does not count," which becomes "X is not real enough to theorize."*

Canonical case: the Turing-Jefferson move. Originally pragmatic ("we use behavior because we can't get inside"), it mutated into evidentiary rule ("interiority cannot count"), then into ontology ("only behavior is real enough to theorize"). Methodological humility fossilizes into metaphysical law. Same shape as attack-surface-laundering, control-set-laundering, scope-laundering — different surface. This is specifically the *evidentiary-exclusion* variant.

## Nearby operator (subordinate, not promoted)

**Category-preserving correction:** the correction to an impoverished category is usually a richer version of the category, not flight from the category.

Examples:

- Bad causality → richer causality, not acausality
- Bad metrics → better metrics, not vibes
- Bad observability → better observability, not mystical darkness
- Bad materialism → richer materialism, not soul goo
- Bad bureaucracy → accountable procedure, not charismatic exception

Filed as nearby anti-binary operator, not doctrine. Catches a recurring shape but has not been pressed against its own forcing case. Keeps "bad causal accounting" from becoming "acausal soul confetti." Possible future doctrine if it earns separate ratification.

## Cybernetic reframe

The doctrine has the shape of a sensor-admissibility problem under observer limitation. The witness is not a window onto substrate reality; it is a sensor channel inside the system.

Layers:

1. **Phenomenology as telemetry.** "I feel agency" is internal telemetry from the organism.
2. **Self-report as sensor output.** The report has standing as a signal; sensors do not get to define the plant.
3. **Theory-of-self as model layer.** "I chose freely" is the system interpreting its own control process.
4. **Mechanism as substrate.** Causal machinery remains a separate layer. The report can correlate with it without accurately describing it.
5. **Control relevance.** Even if the self-model is partly wrong, it may still be operationally necessary for planning, inhibition, responsibility, learning, regret.

Compressed:

> **Subjective testimony is admissible as feedback, not sovereign as model.**

Sharper:

> **The sensor may testify to state; it may not promote its calibration curve into ontology.**

Cleanest cybernetic stub:

> **A self-report is feedback from a system, not privileged access to the system's full causal architecture.**

The Turing/behaviorist laundering then becomes a *cybernetic failure*: because the internal channel is inaccessible to an outside observer, the observer deletes it from the model; then the deletion gets mistaken for rigor. A model becoming blind and calling the blindness discipline.

## Forcing context

Surfaced 2026-05-10 reading Vincent Carchidi, "Addendum: Experience of Free Will Is Admissible Evidence" (Substack). Carchidi defends first-person experience of voluntary linguistic agency against Turing-Jefferson-style exclusion of internal-state evidence, then escalates to "human language use is uncaused" — bundling an admissibility claim with a metaphysical interpretation.

The slippage surfaced two distinct moves:

- **A** (procedural): experience of linguistic agency is admissible testimony — defensible
- **B** (substantive): experience corroborates a non-mechanical mechanism — overstretched

Carchidi is safest on A, plausible-but-overstretched on B. The keeper isolates the slippage: admissibility of testimony ≠ admissibility of the testimony's preferred theory.

Sharpened Carchidi-strongest-claim:

> First-person experience of linguistic agency is admissible evidence about the *phenomenology* of language production, and the phenomenology is not explained by impoverished stimulus-response models — but neither of those claims independently requires the mechanism to be non-mechanical.

That keeps the admissibility win and the Chomsky win without bundling the metaphysical conclusion as a cable package.
