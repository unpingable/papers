**Status:** candidate construction-technique compression, not promoted. Working

**Filed:** 2026-06-02
**Trigger:** Wicket/WLP-Claude survey of the three June 2026 specimens noticed

## The pattern

A recurring kernel pattern is to name an admissibility boundary by:

1. Placing two apparently parallel predicates or surfaces side by side.
2. Proving they are not interchangeable.
3. Quarantining the rule that would collapse them into a separate extension
   (a strictly named DerivableC, PostValidated, raw packet shape, etc.).

The forbidden move is **not** made unrepresentable. It is made non-default,
visible, and unable to masquerade as base admissibility.

Compact form:

> **A laundering rule may be modeled. It may not impersonate the base
> calculus.**

## Why the quarantine matters

Without the quarantined extension, every refusal theorem is cheap. "This cannot
happen because I erased the universe where it happens" is not a boundary; it is
ontology-laundering by silence.

The stronger version is: **the forbidden move is representable, but not
admissible in the base calculus.** That is why `DerivableC` matters, why
`PostValidated` matters, why raw packet shape matters. You do not pretend the
laundering move is impossible. You make it visible, typed, and non-default.

ContractionHinge's Failure Log section is exemplary here — it preserves the
near-miss where additive ∧R (shared context) would have absorbed contraction
into the connective and made T3 false. That preservation is the discipline.

## Editorial criterion for future annexes

If a proposed module only says "bad thing impossible" without representing the
tempting collapse, it may be underpowered. Good annexes show the trapdoor and
then refuse to route ordinary authority through it.

Litmus questions:
- Can a reader point to the predicate or surface that the boundary refuses to
  collapse into?
- Is the forbidden rule defined somewhere in the same module, separated by
  naming and import discipline?
- Would removing the quarantine collapse the no-go theorem?

If all three are yes, the boundary is honest. If any is no, the boundary may be
ontology-locked rather than admissibility-bounded.

## What this is NOT

- Not a meta-theorem. The pattern is a construction-technique observation, not
  a proved property of the kernel.
- Not promotion to `WHAT-THIS-PROVES.md` or any public surface. The pattern is
  craft-altitude, not theorem-altitude.
- Not a license to retrofit "boundary-by-asymmetry" framing onto modules that
  do not exhibit the shape. The discipline is descriptive of an observed
  recurrence, not prescriptive of a required form.
- Not the same compression as the authorization-laundering arc. The arc is
  about *what* the kernel rules out (a topology of four boundaries). The
  pattern is about *how* it rules them out (the construction shape).

## Promotion gates

Same kind of restraint as the arc. Promote from candidate to named doctrine
when:

- A sixth or seventh instance appears that fits the shape without being
  retrofitted to it.
- An attempted kernel addition is rejected for failing the editorial criterion
  (proves "boundary-by-asymmetry" is doing real gating work, not just
  describing past work).
- A consumer cites the pattern as the load-bearing structure for understanding
  the kernel family.

Until then: cite-don't-extract. The instances stand on their own.
