## 0. Thesis (two laws, one candidate)

**Live authority is a monotone, not a conserved quantity.** It is destructible by design:
refusal, revocation, expiry, residue, failed crossing, profile mismatch are sinks. Any
"first law" reading of live authority is false on purpose.

**The receipted delta ledger is the conservation candidate.** Inside a declared calculus:

> **No represented authority delta may be claimed without a typed receipt.**

Two halves:

1. **No silent minting** (proven, repeatedly, v3–v7): free/internal operations cannot
   create or amplify authority. Sources are declared reads/admissions; flux is paid
   bridges/rungs; nothing else increases anything.
2. **No silent disappearance** (mandated by existing doctrine, partially proven as
   equalities): sinks and slack must be typed, receipted, or explicitly named as
   abstention. This half is already on file independently of any conservation framing:
   - `working/refusals-need-receipts.md` — refusal is an accountable act; the
     `RefusalReceipt`; *"absence is never a receipt, in either direction."*
   - `working/tooltheory/relaxation-valve-candidate.md` — de-escalation receipts
     (Caution/Clearance/Waiver); conserved residual-risk budget across live waivers.
   - residue custody (`working/tooltheory/`, residue/coupling incubation) — what is left
     behind is tracked, not dropped.

The conjunction is the candidate law: **conservation = no-minting ∧ no-silent-destruction,
per calculus, conditional on admitted reads.** The corpus mandated the second conjunct
before the conservation framing existed. That is the evidence the framing is latent, not
imposed.

**Scope condition (do not soften):** "no silent disappearance" is a claim about the
*ledger*, not the world. Reality can silently rot standing, keys, custodians, hardware.
The calculus cannot *represent* that change as governed fact until an admission or sink
receipt exists. The read boundary — the world→ledger handshake — is outside every theorem
in the tower. Lean polices amplification; NQ and the observatories police admission. The
demon is still standing at the door; this note does not claim otherwise.

## 2. What the audit is for

The monotone/balance distinction gives every v3–v7 theorem family a checkable question:

> **Is the headline result an equality (balance-grade) or an inequality
> (monotone-grade)? If an inequality, where does the slack go — is it receipted?**

Unreceipted slack is then exactly one of:

1. **a real gap** — a completeness obligation inside opened scope (mandamus violation),
2. **a deliberate abstention** — the calculus names a wall rather than tracking a
   quantity; legitimate, but it must be *named* as abstention, or
3. **an overclaimed family** — the release ledger's language promises more balance than
   the theorem delivers.

All three outputs are useful. None are vibes.

## 5. Candidate theorem schema: `balance_classification`

Per calculus C with operation inductive `Op_C`, verdict type, and local measure(s) μ_C
(possibly a family, per label / per measure function — never a global scalar):

> **Schema.** For every admissible operation (every constructor of `Op_C`, or every input
> to the calculus's decision procedure), the outcome is EXACTLY one of:
>
> 1. **preserve** — μ_C unchanged (equality, per measure);
> 2. **source** — μ_C increases, carrying an admission receipt (read/declared scope);
> 3. **paid flux** — μ_C moves, carrying a bridge/rung/consumption receipt;
> 4. **sink** — μ_C decreases, carrying a typed refusal/expiry/revocation/residue
>    witness (offender named where applicable);
> 5. **named abstention** — the calculus declares it does not track this quantity here.
>
> **No third case / no unreceipted delta:** the disjunction is total and the cases are
> exclusive; no constructor changes μ_C without landing in a receipted branch or a
> declared abstention.

**Adequacy conditions (post-codex, §7.2 findings 1–3 — the schema is unsound without
these):**

- **Receipt adequacy, not receipt presence.** A receipt is admissible in a branch only
  if it *discharges* the delta: the flux/source receipt must carry the local balance
  equation itself (e.g. context = reads + residual, for every measure), and the sink
  receipt must carry the witness inequality (e.g. offender demand genuinely exceeds
  supply). A receipt that merely labels a delta ("because witness, magic") is the §7.1
  minting door and is refused. No `magicBridge : Receipt`.
- **Independence of the operation set.** `Op_C` must predate the thesis — fixed in a
  prior release, no constructors added, no receipt fields added, no abstention branch
  introduced to make totality pass. Totality over an operation set tuned to the theorem
  is vacuous; totality over one fixed two releases earlier is evidence.
- **Per-component classification, no sign discipline on a whole operation.** Real
  operations have mixed deltas (consume one typed resource, emit another, preserve a
  third). The classification is per typed measure/component — in practice: equalities
  quantified over every measure function — never "the operation increased/decreased μ"
  for one ordered μ. That wording pressure is the scalar collapse in disguise.
- **No generic interface.** The schema is prose. Each instance is a local theorem over
  that calculus's own types. A reusable `AuthorityBalance` typeclass with common
  `Op`/`Receipt`/`μ` fields would be the forbidden unifier through the back door; the
  pilot instantiates locally and exports nothing.

The value of the theorem is the **totality under the adequacy conditions**, not the
individual clauses — most clauses already exist (§3). A pile of per-clause lemmas is
bookkeeping; the exhaustive case analysis over a pre-fixed operation inductive, with
delta-discharging receipts, is the law. That is also the honest answer to the "conserved
by definition" objection: yes, every loss got a sink term — *the theorem is that the sink
terms are finitely enumerable, typed, delta-discharging, and cover an operation set that
was not tuned to make them fit.* Same move that made energy conservation contentful after
heat was invented. (Prose analogy only; see §1 fences.)

**Pilot (authorized):** v5 `LinearNormalization` + `OccurrenceTrace`, as
`LeanProofs/Scratch/NoSilentDelta.lean` — statement: every normalization verdict is

systems, quota accounting, double-spend prevention; the fork-modes note already maps

cannot increase; show any increase costs proportional consumption). **Nobody here
discovered resource theory.**

The claimed contribution is narrower — ranked per codex (§7.2 finding 6: the strongest
contribution is corpus-specific):

1. **The audit itself** (§3) — equality-vs-inequality with slack classification as a
   falsifiable pass over an existing corpus, with three named failure outputs and
   predeclared criteria.
2. **Receipt-adequacy accounting of sources and sinks** — not just "monotone under free
   operations" but "every delta lands in a finite, typed case split whose receipts
   *discharge* the delta," with refusal/expiry/revocation/residue as first-class
   receipted sinks rather than unmodeled decay. (Gemini's "authority naturally degrades"
   gloss is the failure mode this clause exists to refuse: monotone ≠ decay; transport ≠
   automatic loss.)
3. **No-global-potential structure** — the law-shape repeats per calculus while the
   no-unifier result forbids the shared scalar. Uniform law without unified judgment.
   In the resource-theory frame the no-go gains teeth: it is not "we failed to unify,"
   it is "a global potential would erase path-sensitive authority" (signature asserts a
   state function; a witness chain is the path integral — prose only).

None of these is claimed as a kernel-level contribution (codex finding 5, conceded): the
formal output of this note is a local corollary/audit theorem per calculus, and the

- **unwitnessed / stale / scope-lost / receiver-ambiguous hop** → admissibility break or
  sink;

custody across it. Keeper:

> **Transit is not decay. Transit is a demand for custody accounting.**
