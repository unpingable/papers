## Status legend

- **current-load-bearing** — actively cited from doctrine map / MEMORY.md / current-session work. Cite freely.
- **active-scratch** — in-flight work (candidate kernels, codex review logs, ongoing investigations). Cite with awareness that the conclusion may move.
- **draft-paper** — paper-shaped artifact in progress. NOT yet a preprint.
- **reference-route-sign** — map, index, or positioning note. Points at other artifacts; does NOT carry independent doctrine claim.
- **specimen** — formal-shaped illustrative artifact (Alloy probe, worked example). Cited as instance, not as primitive.
- **superseded** — work that's been replaced or absorbed; the canonical content now lives elsewhere. Cite the successor instead.
- **stale-by-vocabulary** — uses retired vocabulary (calculus naming, pre-2026-06-03 discipline). Content may still be valuable but vocabulary will mislead a current reader. Cite carefully and translate vocabulary.
- **retired-dead-letter** — explicitly retired; preserved for provenance only. Do NOT cite as current authority.
- **reference-foundational** — older file (often May 10 vintage) that the README treats as substrate / cognitive-frame foundation. Doctrine may have evolved around it but the framing is still in active use. Cite as background, not as cutting-edge.
- **unknown** — I haven't engaged with this file enough this week to classify honestly. Needs a read pass before next citation.
