## Frame

The problem in compressed form:

> **ACAB but you need C.**

Necessary coercion without allowing coercion to become a caste. Coercion is real (bodies, liberty, standing, custody, exclusion, force can be at stake). Permanent coercive identity is the failure mode. The architecture asks: what would it look like to keep the function and refuse the caste?

The compression is a joke. The doctrine is not. Per [[feedback-jokes-as-compression]], translate before relying.

**Scope fence (load-bearing):**

> This note describes containment conditions for coercive authority, not sufficient conditions for justice.

The architecture is a constraint pattern. Following it does not, by itself, make coercion morally legitimate. Leased/logged/split/sunset is design discipline, not absolution with paperwork.

## Core doctrine

> **Coercion must be leased, logged, split, and sunset.**

> **Necessary coercion must never become a permanent identity class.**

## Constraint pattern (coercive offices)

When an institution can touch bodies, liberty, standing, custody, exclusion, or force:

- authority is leased, not owned
- scope is incident-bound or function-bound
- action leaves receipts
- offices are split across incompatible powers
- exceptions trigger review
- continuation requires renewal
- no office may inherit what it dismantles

## Architectural moves

### 1. Incident-scoped authority

Crisis response authority activates only for the incident. It expires by default. The responder does not "own safety" in the community.

### 2. Role firewalls

```
Immediate crisis stabilization ≠ investigation
Investigation              ≠ adjudication
Adjudication               ≠ custody
Custody                    ≠ treatment
Treatment                  ≠ surveillance
Surveillance               ≠ future standing authority
```

Each `≠` is a structural refusal, not a personnel constraint. The same human may rotate through roles in their career; the offices must not collapse into a single role.

### 3. Exception receipts

Unavoidable observations during incident response can trigger a new process. They cannot expand the old role.

**Anti-cultivation rule:** no outside body may ask responders to "keep an eye out next time." Surveillance is not a side-effect of crisis response.

### 4. Handoff as event, not relationship

Coordination is logged, scoped, time-bound, and auditable. No informal interagency priesthood.

> **Professional familiarity is an attack surface.**

### 5. Stable competence, unstable authority

Professionalize the craft. Deprofessionalize the jurisdiction. Let skill persist; let coercive office expire.

This is the load-bearing inversion of the standard professionalization argument. Standard form: "more training, more professional standards, more career stability." Inverted form: "yes to all three for *skill*, no to all three for *jurisdiction-over-bodies*."

### 6. Memory in artifacts, not lore

Continuity belongs in records, training simulations, after-action review, protocols. Not in "everybody knows that guy / that house / that family."

Lore is the substrate of admissibility decay across generations of officers.

### 7. Split back office

No single body owns training, dispatch, records, review, consequence, and exception escrow.

```
standards body
credentialing body
dispatch body
evidence custodian
escrow/review body
due-process body
```

The split is structural, not merely organizational. Each body has its own admissibility scope; no body may aggregate scopes.

### 8. Transition receivership

Transition authority must be scaffolding, not sovereignty. **No body authorized to dismantle a power may keep the power it dismantles.** Functional displacement, not reform garnish: move a call category out of the old police stack entirely and deny re-entry.

This is the architecture's hardest invariant. It is also where reform almost always fails. A "transition team" that becomes the new permanent body has reproduced the failure mode at one remove.

### 9. Public legitimacy layer

One front door, many firewalled offices, public receipts, no secret king. Public face is router/narrator/ledger, not sovereign.

> **Just don't give the phone number a gun, a database, a mythology, and budget incentives.**

### Final doctrine candidate

> **One front door. Many firewalled offices. Public receipts. No secret king.**

## Kernel-overlap audit

The architecture composes substantial pieces of the admissibility kernel into an applied scenario. Mapping:

- **`FiatAdmissibility`** (artifact-kind × use-kind) — "no office may inherit what it dismantles" is a kind-axis claim. The transition body's artifact-kind is `receiver` / `scaffold`; using it as `sovereign` is unauthorized substitution. Same shape as fiat-doesn't-govern-state-mutation, applied to coercive offices.
- **`SurfaceAuthorization`** — the public legitimacy layer is exactly a SurfaceAuthorization construction: a surface that is *not* the sovereign. "Public face is router/narrator/ledger" is the same architectural move as "steward is notary, not legislator" from the civic migration appliance. Different domain, same surface-vs-authority cut.
- **`Signal Authority`** (working/signal-authority-candidate.md) — exception receipts defend against null-laundering across role boundaries. Observations during incident response do not license expanded authority by silence-as-consent.
- **`Commitment Standing Decay`** (working/commitment-standing-decay-candidate.md) — the architecture's whole concern is the *prevention* of decay-resistant license: licenses that should expire but don't. Decay family is about stale licenses; this architecture is about preventing licenses from becoming structurally permanent in the first place. Inverse-axis sibling.
- **`non-reciprocal admissibility flow`** (working/non-reciprocal-admissibility-flow.md) — "professional familiarity is an attack surface" and the anti-cultivation rule are direct NRAF instances. Officers shape civilians' admissible futures via informal relationships with no reciprocal accountability, presented as community-policing or relationship-building rather than authority. Same five-question pattern: directional, no reciprocal standing, no contestability, surface neutrality, state consequence.
- **Controller continuity** (project-controller-continuity) — "handoff as event, not relationship" maps directly to controller-continuity's handoff failure family. The architecture is preventing controller-continuity from becoming a relationship-of-power.
- **Governor doctrine** (project-governor-doctrine) — authority-boundary instantiation. Coercive offices are an authority-boundary application; agent_gov is the technical sibling.
- **Register capture as admissibility failure** (project-register-capture-admissibility) — "memory in artifacts, not lore" defends against register capture across generations. Lore is a substrate-axis drift channel for what counts as admissible coercive action.

The architecture is *not* a new primitive. It is a composite, like the CMA. Composition pressure on the kernel: useful audit for which primitives bend uncomfortably when forced into this domain.

## Embedded candidate handles (named, not promoted)

Three structural moves in the architecture are sharp enough to be candidate primitives in their own right. Filed here for future reference, *not promoted* — they need a second forcing case in another domain before they earn standalone status.

### 1. Stable competence, unstable authority

The distinction between *skill* (continuous, professionalized, persists) and *authority-to-coerce* (discontinuous, leased, expires). This is not in the existing kernel.

Generator test (informal): does the move depend on collapsing skill-continuity and authority-continuity into a single career structure? If yes, the architecture refuses the collapse. If no, the architecture has nothing to say.

Multi-domain candidates: medical professionals (procedural skill continuous, prescribing authority leased), judges (legal expertise continuous, jurisdiction term-limited), regulatory inspectors (technical expertise continuous, enforcement authority delegated per investigation).

### 2. Anti-cultivation rule

> No outside body may ask the responder to maintain observation across incidents.

Sharper than "surveillance is bad." This rule says: even where surveillance might be operationally useful, the request to surveil *across incidents* — cultivation of ongoing watchfulness — is structurally refused at the institutional level. The responder may not be recruited into an informal intelligence apparatus by accumulation of asks.

Sibling to NRAF (the cultivated relationship has the NRAF shape) but operationally distinct: this is a refusal-rule, not a description of a failure mode.

### 3. Transition receivership / no-self-inheritance

> No body authorized to dismantle a power may keep the power it dismantles.

This is the architecture's hardest claim. It generalizes beyond coercive offices: any body with dismantling authority is structurally prevented from being the successor body. Closely related to FiatAdmissibility's artifact-kind × use-kind axis (dismantler kind ≠ successor kind), but the temporal/causal shape is distinctive.

Multi-domain candidates: bankruptcy receivers (cannot become permanent operators), constitutional conventions (cannot become permanent governments), regulatory remediation orders (orderer cannot become the regulated), audit firms (cannot become the audited entity's permanent finance department).

## Anti-collapse risks

Per the anti-collapse runbook memory ([[feedback-anti-collapse-runbook]]). Named failure modes for this architecture:

1. **Field minting.** "Leased Coercion" sounds like it could become a discipline. It is not. It is a governance pattern that composes existing admissibility primitives. Resist treating it as a new field with its own conferences and certifications. (The certification body would itself be the target of the architecture's split-back-office rule.)
2. **Universal solvent.** The lease/log/split/sunset pattern is generic enough to be applied to everything ("software permissions are leased coercion!"). Useful when it points at real structure (regulatory enforcement, immigration, child welfare, mental-health holds). Diluting when applied to anything with revocable scope.
3. **ACAB-baggage capture.** The opening frame ("ACAB but you need C") is political vocabulary that may shape reception more than the architecture itself. The structural moves work across many political contexts — domestic policing, immigration enforcement, child welfare, mental-health holds, regulatory enforcement, private corporate security. ACAB framing closes some doors before reaching them. The architecture should survive translation into less politically loaded vocabulary, or it is not yet architectural.
4. **Reform garnish.** The architecture could be appropriated *as* the reform that prevents real change. "We've adopted leased-coercion principles!" while the substance remains intact. The transition-receivership invariant defends against this specifically, but the architecture *as a whole* could be weaponized this way. Watch for adoption-without-functional-displacement.
5. **Cube poisoning** (anti-collapse runbook). If synthesized too aggressively, any failure of policing becomes "they didn't implement leased coercion." That is universal-solvent at the legitimacy layer. The architecture is a *pattern for design*, not a *test for legitimacy of existing institutions*.
6. **Mirror poisoning** (anti-collapse runbook). Constant talk of "the priesthood," "the caste," "the secret king" may reify the failure mode rather than dispel it. Use the diagnostic vocabulary sparingly; foreground the constructive moves.
7. **Jokes-as-doctrine.** "ACAB but you need C" is good compression, not doctrine. "How not to re-invent cops" is good compression, not a title. Per [[feedback-jokes-as-compression]] — translate to the serious claim before filing the joke as load-bearing.
8. **Premature meta-pattern synthesis.** The shape rhymes with the civic migration appliance (bounded stewardship of community governance; leased coercion as bounded constraint of coercive office). The rhyme may be real or may be coincidence. Resist drafting a "bounded authority across domains" meta-paper. Each capture stands; the meta-pattern earns its keep only by accumulating instances outside the originating cluster.

## What this is not

- Not a policy proposal.
- Not a reform program.
- Not an abolitionist platform.
- Not a defense of coercive institutions.
- Not a critique of any specific implementation.
- Not a paper or paper-candidate (yet).
- Not a specification (yet).
- Not a primitive (and the embedded candidates are not yet either).

It is a structural capture of an architecture that surfaced in conversation: what coercion would have to look like to be necessary and not become a caste. The capture exists to prevent re-derivation. The architecture's deployment, defense, or critique is downstream of capture.
