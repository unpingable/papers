## Keeper

> The ontology is obvious, provided you already survived the ontology.

Mechanism in one line:

> Post-orientation artifacts presented as entry-point artifacts.

## Working definition

Orientation Debt is the accumulation of missing entry conditions in an artifact, system, or theory such that the artifact is legible only to people who have already internalized the ontology required to read it.

## Failure predicate

Orientation Debt is in play when:

> A capable cold reader cannot tell what the artifact is, what problem it addresses, or what reading path to follow without reconstructing the author's internal ontology from scattered traces.

The diagnostic mark: the artifact is *coherent*, often impressively so, but coherence is recoverable only by readers willing to do the entrance-path reconstruction the artifact omits. Cold readers tend to mistake density for crankery because the orientation surface has been removed, not because the underlying work is bad.

## Mechanism

The artifact is built from inside a fully stabilized conceptual frame. The author can no longer see the frame as something one enters; they see it as the air. Entry conditions that were once load-bearing are gradually compressed out as redundant. The result reads natively to the author and to anyone else inside the frame, and progressively less to anyone arriving from outside.

The compression is rarely intentional. It is the natural consequence of writing forward from a stabilized vocabulary without keeping a back-channel to the pre-stabilization state.

| **Stale-binding family** | state outlives its justifying premise | different surface — stale binding is about authority/state going stale; orientation debt is about access paths going stale |

The shared family shape across the three is *the path that made X usable is gone.* The surfaces differ: design-basis erasure on the artifact's hazard model; apprenticeship substrate erosion on the people-pipeline; orientation debt on the reader-entrance. Same family, distinct primitives.

## Symptoms

- README begins with internal vocabulary before purpose.
- Bridge documents exist but require already knowing they exist to find them.
- Status registers and audit interfaces are useful only after the reader has internalized the status ontology.
- The system is coherent, but coherence is discoverable only after undue reverse engineering.
- Cold readers mistake density for crankery because the entrance path has been removed.
- "Start here" assumes the reader already knows what *here* is.

## Examples

- The lean and papers repositories before the 2026-05-01 cold-read pass: stubborn readers could decode the project; casual readers bounced. Fixed by adding audit-story openings, explicit companion-repo blocks, and a public vestibule (`docs/index.md`).
- Any framework whose vocabulary is tuned to its own internal load-bearing distinctions and presents that vocabulary as if it were a starting point.
- Specifications written from inside a stabilized governance frame that omit the orientation path that justified the frame's vocabulary in the first place.
- Academic field-entry literature where the introduction begins by negotiating boundaries with adjacent fields rather than telling a newcomer what the object is.

## Promotion criterion

Promote from `active candidate` to full primitive only after the failure mode appears in at least two genuinely non-authorial domains — e.g. project docs at other organizations, institutional process documentation, third-party software onboarding, API docs, governance specs, academic field-entry literature. The current ratification is one domain (the user's own work). One domain is enough to file; two is the gate for promotion.

The reason for restraint: the primitive describes a real and recurrent failure shape, but it also describes one of the easiest framings to over-deploy as universal critique. Self-referential ivy. Hold at active-candidate until the cross-domain pressure earns the promotion honestly.
