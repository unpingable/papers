## Reframe — habitat is not genus

**Prosecutorial decomposition is not "online dunk logic."** It is an old evidentiary/inferential failure that social media *industrializes*. The platform-discourse specimens are the current high-throughput substrate; the underlying genus is centuries older and lives in courtrooms, hospitals, performance reviews, peer review, audits, and households.

*Social media is just the vape pen. The nicotine is ancient.*

Earlier draft of this file framed the failure as platform-metabolism. That framing was too narrow: it locates the pathology in an institution that merely accelerates a much older error. The correction: name the genus first, treat platform discourse as one specimen domain among many.

## Two names

- **Specificity-to-attack-surface conversion** — the diagnostic name (mechanism-side). What the operation *is*.
- **Prosecutorial decomposition** — the social-behavior name (audience-side). What the audience *does*.

Use the diagnostic name when explaining the mechanism. Use the social-behavior name when naming the audience action.

## Genus statement

> A true particular is treated as an authorized warrant for a whole-object judgment, without the inference-rule (the "bridge") that would license the lift.

The lift is the failure. The particular may be true. The whole-object judgment may even, in the end, be correct. But the *route from particular to judgment* is the audit point. PD names the failure mode where that route is unbuilt and the lift happens anyway.

## Lineage anchor — prosecutor's fallacy (close cousin)

**The closest doctrinal anchor is prosecutor's fallacy**, but precision matters: prosecutor's fallacy is specifically *probability inversion* — confusing $P(E \mid H)$ with $P(H \mid E)$. Example: "The probability of this DNA match if the defendant is innocent is 1 in a million, therefore the probability of innocence is 1 in a million." That's a Bayesian conditional error.

PD is *broader*: it's fragment-to-whole warrant laundering, whether or not probability is involved. The hair-match → guilty inference is PD-shaped; the Bayesian-inversion version is the prosecutor's-fallacy sub-case. PD includes prosecutor's fallacy as one mechanism but covers many other lift-failures that are not Bayesian (categorical, evaluative, causal, character-based).

> **Prosecutor's fallacy is the load-bearing cousin. It is not the parent.**

Other ancestors / cognates:
- **Evidence-law materiality and sufficiency doctrines** — what fragments are admissible *for which uses*; how much evidence is required for a given judgment.
- **Tversky-Kahneman representativeness heuristic** — instances treated as typical without sampling-frame audit.
- **Philosophy of science on partial falsification** — flaw-in-derivation vs. fatal-to-theory; which fragments are load-bearing.
- **Differential diagnosis discipline** — single finding treated as diagnosis without ruling alternatives in/out.

The doctrinal ancestors are *deeper than discourse criticism*. They are the actual home.

## The five bridge types

The structural spine. Each PD specimen is a case where exactly one (or more) of these bridges was *required but not built* before the fragment was used to convict the whole:

1. **Materiality bridge** — *does this fragment matter to the whole claim?*
   The fragment is true, but its relation to the whole-object judgment hasn't been established. (Legal-doctrine origin.)

2. **Representativeness bridge** — *is this instance typical, exceptional, cherry-picked, or structurally diagnostic?*
   The fragment may be a fair sample, a single outlier, an adversarial pick, or a structural canary. The lift behaves differently in each case. (Statistical / cognitive-science origin.)

3. **Causal bridge** — *does this fragment cause the alleged whole failure, or merely accompany it?*
   Temporal coincidence or correlation treated as causal warrant. (Philosophy-of-science origin.)

4. **Sufficiency bridge** — *is this enough evidence for the judgment being made?*
   The fragment may genuinely matter and genuinely correlate, and still fall short of the evidentiary threshold for the use being made of it. (Legal-doctrine origin — "preponderance" vs. "clear and convincing" vs. "beyond reasonable doubt" gradient.)

5. **Standing bridge** — *is the forum/actor allowed to convert this fact into that consequence?*
   The fragment may be true, material, representative, causal, and sufficient — and still not be authorized for use by *this actor in this forum*. (Legal-doctrine origin — jurisdictional / procedural standing.)

> **Fragment truth is admissible at fragment scope. Whole judgment requires an authorized bridge.**

Bridge taxonomy is the structural payload. Specimens get tagged by which bridge(s) are missing.

## Mechanism — what gets stripped

A claim with governing structure (hedges, scope qualifiers, named limitations, evidentiary thresholds) is decomposed into its component fragments. Each fragment is then deployed as if it were a freestanding warrant for whole-object judgment. The governing structure was the bridge-machinery that made the fragments honest in context. Stripping it produces an *unauthorized lift*.

Crucially: the fragments retain their wording. What they lose is the bridge-machinery. This is *not* compression failure (where detail is flattened); it is **bridge stripping**. The fragments look more credible than the whole because they preserve specificity while shedding the structure that disciplines specificity.

## Worked example (the operator-originating specimen)

A careful claim like:

> *"The Dem professional apparatus tends to preserve failed consultants more reliably than it disciplines them, though there are local actors and policy wins that complicate the picture."*

gets decomposed into attackable surfaces — each one demanding a different bridge that the original speaker never claimed to have built:

- "apparatus" — *requires standing bridge*: does the speaker have authority to characterize a network as an "apparatus"?
- "failed consultants" — *requires materiality bridge*: which consultants, on what failure criteria, for what claim scope?
- "more reliably" — *requires sufficiency bridge*: what comparison class, what data, what threshold?
- "disciplines" — *requires representativeness bridge*: do counter-examples exist, and are they typical or rare?
- "tends" — *requires sufficiency bridge*: how strong a tendency, against what null?
- "professional" / "Dem" — *requires materiality bridge*: are these terms doing argumentative work or are they incidental?

Each demand is, *in isolation*, the kind of refinement a careful interlocutor might raise. The PD failure is the **conjunctive prosecution**: each fragment treated as a freestanding charge requiring complete bridge-construction, with the cumulative burden equal to "defend every word against every possible objection." The hedges in the original ("tends," "local actors," "complicate the picture") were the speaker's own honest acknowledgment that bridges were partial. The decomposition strips the acknowledgment and prosecutes the partiality.

## Cross-examination as the law-shaped limiting case

Legitimate cross-examination *does* fragment impeachment under rules. The bridge structure already exists in evidence law: *materiality doctrine* gates whether a fragment-level inconsistency reaches a whole-witness judgment; *materially inconsistent* impeachment may degrade specific testimony, but only collateral-evidence rules govern whether it impeaches the witness wholesale.

The PD failure is the *unauthorized lift*: fragment-level impeachment laundered into whole-witness conviction *without invoking the materiality machinery the law already requires*. The clean test: if a defense attorney did this in court, opposing counsel would object on materiality grounds and the judge would sustain.

This matters as a guard against misuse of the PD handle: *decomposition is not always invalid*. The handle names the unauthorized lift, not all fragment-level engagement.

## Anti-promotion and anti-misuse guards

- **Do not promote to primitive without specimens distributed across the bridge taxonomy.** A specimen catalog dominated by one missing-bridge type (e.g., only sufficiency failures) doesn't earn ratification; PD's claim is genus-level, which requires cross-bridge coverage.
- **Do not formalize the decomposition algebra as PD-specific.** The Lean-shaped residue is the broader bridge-rule machinery (see Lean deferral). PD itself is applied vocabulary, not kernel.
- **Do not pair-mint with ASL yet.** Paired-primitive status (the way ASL paired with Gate-to-Metric Substitution) requires multiple forcing cases.
- **Do not let "prosecutorial decomposition" become dismissal vocabulary for legitimate cross-examination, peer review, audit, or postmortem.** Each of these institutions has built-in bridge-rule machinery. Calling their licit use "PD" launders the handle into anti-scrutiny rhetoric — the opposite of what it names.
- **Do not absorb every fragment-based criticism into PD.** Discriminator: *was the bridge stripped, or was it never claimed?* A speaker who never offered a governing frame is not subject to PD; they may be subject to other critiques (unfalsifiability, vagueness). PD requires that the speaker built a bridge and the audience stripped it.

## Lean deferral

ChatGPT's spike (2026-05-21) proposed a Lean sketch:

```lean
inductive UseKind where | orientation | impeachment | conviction

structure FragmentEvidence (Whole Fragment : Type) where
  fragment : Fragment
  true_of_fragment : Prop

structure BridgeRule (Whole Fragment : Type) where
  lifts_to_whole : Fragment → Whole → Prop

def AdmissibleUse … (UseKind.conviction, none) => False …

theorem fragment_truth_does_not_convict_without_bridge … :
  ¬ AdmissibleUse UseKind.conviction ev whole none := by simp [AdmissibleUse]
```

**Three audit findings prevent landing this as-written:**

1. **Tautology.** `AdmissibleUse conviction ev whole none` unfolds *by definition* to `False`. The theorem is `¬ False`, proved by definitional unfolding. Conclusion-shaped predicate — encodes the claim and re-derives it from its own encoding. A non-trivial theorem requires a model with two distinct predicates (`evidence_admissibility ev ev.fragment` and `whole_authority ev whole`) and an *existence* result: some fragments are evidence-admissible but not whole-authority-admissible.
2. **Redundant constructor.** `UseKind.impeachment` and `UseKind.conviction` both require bridge with no other distinction. The third case carries no computational content. Either trim to two cases or build the differential bridge-strength machinery (impeachment-with-weak-bridge succeeds, conviction-with-weak-bridge fails) that earns the third case.
3. **Unresolved kernel-overlap.** The proposed module is plausibly an instance of (or extension of) SurfaceAuthorization. Per [[feedback-kernel-overlap-audit]], landing a new kernel module requires that audit. Not yet run.

**Reframe (load-bearing):** the kernel candidate is not `ProsecutorialDecomposition.lean`. It is the broader machinery — `FragmentEvidence` + `BridgeRule` + `UseKind` + `UnauthorizedLift` — of which PD is one *applied named pattern*. This makes PD downstream applied vocabulary; the kernel question is about the bridge-rule machinery.

The bridge-rule machinery probably belongs as a small extension to SurfaceAuthorization or FiatAdmissibility, *not* a new sibling kingdom. The five-bridge taxonomy (materiality / representativeness / causality / sufficiency / standing) suggests the machinery is real and reusable.

**Gate before any Lean lands:**
1. SurfaceAuthorization-reduction audit: does the bridge-rule machinery reduce to SurfaceAuthorization + a frame-stripping operator, or does it have irreducible additional structure?
2. Downstream consumer identified: who in the kernel (or in tool code at agent_gov / Wicket / NQ) would pattern-match on `UseKind` or `BridgeRule`? If nothing downstream consumes it, the module is decorative.
3. Tautology problem fixed: the theorem statement must be non-trivial — an existence result, not a definitional unfolding.

Until all three gates clear, no Lean module. Working-note vocabulary only.

**Sharpened prior (2026-05-21, per [[feedback-kernel-overlap-audit]] keeper):** The bridge-rule machinery probably reduces to `SurfaceAuthorization + frame-stripping operator + bridge-kind taxonomy`. The five-bridge taxonomy (materiality / representativeness / causality / sufficiency / standing) is the only candidate file-worthy residue, and it is *probably doctrine/tooling vocabulary rather than Lean.* Lean already knows *valid local surface ≠ authorized global lift* via SurfaceAuthorization; the bridge-kinds discriminate *which kind of renewal* a given lift would need, but they don't introduce a new obligation shape the kernel doesn't already carry. Promotion to Lean module only if the audit finds an obligation shape the existing kernel doesn't own. Default expectation: this stays prose/recognition vocabulary indefinitely; PD becomes applied name for *frame-stripping in front of SurfaceAuthorization*, not a Lean kingdom.

## Promotion gate

A move from candidate handle to ratified primitive requires:

1. **Specimens distributed across the five bridge types** — not just sufficiency or just materiality failures, but a spread that demonstrates the bridge taxonomy is genuinely structural and not a single failure dressed in five outfits.
2. **At least one paper or essay draft makes PD load-bearing** — communication of a claim requires the handle and no existing primitive carries it.
3. **Composition audit names sibling relations to ASL, LGVG, CBA, SurfaceAuthorization without collapsing into any of them.** Currently passes for ASL/LGVG/CBA; pending for SurfaceAuthorization.
4. **Bridge-rule kernel machinery audit** (the Lean-reframe question) resolves to either "small extension to existing kernel" or "irreducible new structure earning its own module." Until then, kernel claims remain working-vocabulary.

If none of these fire, the entry stays as recognition vocabulary in the watchlist. Success state, not failure.
