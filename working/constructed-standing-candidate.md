## 1. The finding

> **Not: can we make authority constructible?
> Yes: the corpus already constructs standing judgments under declared roots.**

The corpus was a construction calculus all along, wearing its negative results as
walls. The sequent rules read forward are constructors; the negative theorems prevent
false constructors. What was missing was not an object but a *reading*: the positive
face was never named as the teaching object. Current public posture reads as a wall of
"you may not" — mint, silently delta, read standing for free. The positive statement
the corpus already proves:

> Standing may be constructed without minting: read-rooted genealogy, typed
> derivation, support chain, declared scope, governed readout.

## 5. No legitimacy promise

The money analogy ("a recognized issuer may construct") walks straight into the
regress: *who authorizes the mint?* The corpus already answered and the positive
framing must not un-answer it: genealogy roots in a **stipulated root**
(`NoFreeStandingReadout`: standing is empty without one; root stipulation =
validity-under-declared-order). The kernel cannot manufacture legitimacy; it can force
arbitrary power to sign its own name. Constructed standing is construction *under a
declared order* — the construction chain proves custody of the issuance, never that the
issuer deserved to issue.

## 6. Falsifier

This reading is empty if the positive face does not actually cover what it claims: if
some resident calculus constructs standing-bearing judgments through a path NOT
covered by §2's map (a constructor with no genealogy theorem, or a genealogy theorem
with no closed-routes guard), then the "positive face" is partial and this note
overclaims. That check is cheap — it is the audit-table pass (conservation note §3)
read constructor-side — and worth running whenever a new calculus ships.

**Keeper:** *Authority is not constructed by looking. Standing is constructed by
posting, supporting, and admitting.*
