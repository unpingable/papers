# Plain-language summary

**Companion to:** *Premature Commitment, Belated Consequence, and the Empty Binding Window* (Δt Paper 26)

**Version/status:** v1.0, published on Zenodo

**Reading status:** This is an explanatory companion to the paper, not the canonical published artifact. It does not silently amend the version of record and introduces no new paper claims.

Some decisions fail because a process is slow. Others fail because the system can only know enough to bind after binding would no longer matter. Treating both as throughput problems sends reform toward more staff, faster intake, or better tooling even when no feasible speedup can make legitimacy and consequence overlap.

The paper models two changing conditions. **Admissibility maturity** asks whether the evidence, deliberation, and standing needed for a binding decision have developed. **Consequence viability** asks whether that decision can still produce its intended effect. Their overlap is the valid binding window. Acting before maturity is premature commitment; acting after consequences have expired is belated consequence; and no overlap at all creates an **empty binding window**.

The distinction changes the remedy. Ordinary delay may respond to more capacity or faster execution. An empty window requires changing the authority structure, burden of proof, entitlement, consequence timing, or the event being bound. To avoid labeling every bad queue “structural,” the paper requires analysts to show that a gap remains after feasible process improvements and to state what feasibility regime they assumed. It provides a test for when optimization is enough and when the decision geometry itself has to change.

## Claim boundary

The maturity and consequence thresholds come from the governing standard and purpose of a case; the paper does not provide universal numeric thresholds. Regime choice is politically consequential and cannot be automated away. Some cases change subtype over time, and the boundary between improving an operating point and changing a curve remains rough. Candidate refinements for politically reproduced failures and phase-specific subtypes are explicitly not promoted. Later formal work does not prove these curves, clocks, windows, or classifications.
