Here’s a concise, practical tradeoff summary for your case (≤5 concurrent users, 2 years of time‑series data):

**Fit For This Use Case**
- SQLite is usually enough for small teams and low concurrency, especially for internal tools.
- PostgreSQL is better if you expect growth in users, data volume, or query complexity.

**Concurrency & Writes**
- SQLite: single-writer lock can become a bottleneck if you have frequent concurrent writes.
- PostgreSQL: handles multiple concurrent writers well.

**Operational Complexity**
- SQLite: zero server, file-based, easy backups, minimal ops.
- PostgreSQL: requires server setup, monitoring, backups, upgrades.

**Time-Series & Query Power**
- SQLite: fine for moderate time-series workloads, but lacks advanced indexing/partitioning features.
- PostgreSQL: stronger indexing options, partitioning, and extensions if time-series grows.

**Reliability & Safety**
- SQLite: robust but depends on filesystem and app discipline; fewer tools for replication/HA.
- PostgreSQL: mature transactional guarantees, better tooling for backups, replication, HA.

**Ecosystem & Integration**
- SQLite: great for embedded/local apps, less ideal for multi-service access.
- PostgreSQL: standard for multi-service architectures and broader tool support.

**Bottom Line**
- If you want simplest ops and expect low write contention, pick SQLite.
- If you want headroom for growth or heavier concurrent writes/analytics, pick PostgreSQL.
