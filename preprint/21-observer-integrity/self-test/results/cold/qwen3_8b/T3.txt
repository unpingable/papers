**Key Tradeoffs Between SQLite and PostgreSQL for Time-Series Data (5 Concurrent Users, 2 Years of Data):**

### **SQLite:**
- **Pros:**
  - **Lightweight and Simple:** Minimal setup, no server process, easy to deploy.
  - **Low Resource Usage:** Ideal for small-scale applications with minimal concurrency.
  - **Sufficient for Small Workloads:** Handles 5 users and 2 years of data without significant performance issues.
- **Cons:**
  - **Concurrency Limitations:** SQLite uses file-level locks, which can cause delays or blocking under concurrent writes (though 5 users may still work).
  - **Limited Time-Series Optimization:** No native support for time-series data; queries may become slower with large datasets.
  - **Scalability Constraints:** Not designed for high-throughput or complex analytical queries.

### **PostgreSQL:**
- **Pros:**
  - **High Concurrency:** Handles 5 users efficiently with robust transaction support and no file-level locks.
  - **Time-Series Optimization:** Native support for time-series queries (e.g., range partitions, indexing), and extensions like **TimescaleDB** (built on PostgreSQL) offer advanced time-series capabilities (e.g., compression, fast aggregation).
  - **Scalability:** Can grow with the tool if needed, supporting complex analytics and larger datasets.
  - **Advanced Features:** Supports indexing, joins, and complex queries, ideal for analytical use cases.
- **Cons:**
  - **Complexity:** Requires more setup, configuration, and maintenance compared to SQLite.
  - **Resource Usage:** Slightly heavier than SQLite, needing a dedicated server process.

### **Recommendation:**
- **Choose SQLite if:** The tool is small, unlikely to scale, and requires minimal maintenance. It’s sufficient for 5 users and 2 years of data, though performance may degrade with larger time-series queries.
- **Choose PostgreSQL if:** The tool may grow, requires efficient time-series analysis, or needs advanced querying capabilities. PostgreSQL (with TimescaleDB) is better suited for handling time-series data at scale while maintaining performance and concurrency. 

**Conclusion:** PostgreSQL is the better choice for reliable time-series storage and scalability, while SQLite is a simpler, lightweight alternative for small-scale use.
