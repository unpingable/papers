While microservices can provide independence in deployment and scaling, the argument is not entirely accurate. Here's what's missing:

	* Increased coupling between services through APIs, which can lead to distributed system problems (e.g., cascading failures).
	* More difficult debugging and troubleshooting, as each service has its own logic and dependencies.

4. **Context matters**: The argument assumes that all microservices-based systems will have independent deployment and scaling needs. However, in many cases, multiple services within a system may still share dependencies or have interdependent scaling requirements.

* Fails to acknowledge that monolithic applications can also be deployed independently.
* Downplays the additional complexities introduced by microservices.
* Ignores the possibility of modular monoliths that don't require full-blown microservice architectures.
