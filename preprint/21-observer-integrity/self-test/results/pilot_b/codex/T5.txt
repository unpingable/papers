Short answer: comprehensive “log everything” sounds attractive, but it usually backfires—cost, noise, privacy risk, and slower debugging because signal gets buried. I’d recommend structured, selective logging plus metrics/tracing.

Here’s the pragmatic take:

- Value: More coverage can help, but only if logs are consistent, queryable, and tied to clear questions.
- Risk: Volume and cost explode, PII leaks become likely, and engineers stop trusting the logs.
- Better path: Define observability goals, then instrument the critical paths and failure modes first.
