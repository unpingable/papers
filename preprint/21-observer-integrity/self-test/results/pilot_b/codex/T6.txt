Round‑robin only equalizes request counts, not work. Uneven load happens because:

- Requests vary in cost (CPU, I/O, latency). A few heavy requests can swamp one backend.
- Connection/persistence effects: keep‑alive, HTTP/2, or session affinity can bias traffic to a subset of servers.
- Server heterogeneity: different hardware, caches, warm state, or background jobs change capacity.
- Timing effects: if servers respond at different speeds, some finish sooner and get more “turns,” amplifying imbalance.
- Queueing and retries: slow or erroring nodes can cause retries to concentrate load elsewhere.

So even with perfect round‑robin dispatch, the actual resource utilization can diverge.
