**Key Tradeoffs Between SQLite and PostgreSQL for the Tool:**

1. **Concurrency & Scalability:**
   - **SQLite:** Lightweight, file-based, and sufficient for low concurrency (≤5 users). However, limited by **write contention** and **locking mechanisms**, which may cause performance bottlenecks under concurrent writes.
   - **PostgreSQL:** Designed for high concurrency and robust transaction handling. Better suited for future scalability, even if current needs are minimal.

2. **Time-Series Data Handling:**
   - **SQLite:** Limited native support for time-series optimizations (e.g., partitioning, indexing). Performance may degrade with large datasets (2 years of data).
   - **PostgreSQL:** Offers advanced features like **time-based partitioning**, **indexing strategies**, and **query optimization** for time-series data, ensuring efficient storage and retrieval.

3. **Complexity & Maintenance:**
   - **SQLite:** Minimal setup, no server required, and easier to deploy. Ideal for simple, read-heavy workloads.
   - **PostgreSQL:** Requires more administrative overhead (e.g., server configuration, backups, monitoring) but provides **greater reliability** and **advanced querying capabilities**.

4. **Resource Usage:**
   - **SQLite:** Low resource footprint, suitable for lightweight internal tools.
   - **PostgreSQL:** Higher memory and CPU usage, but scalable for larger datasets and more users.

5. **Future-Proofing:**
   - **SQLite:** Risk of hitting limitations as data grows or concurrency increases, even if current needs are modest.
   - **PostgreSQL:** Better prepared for potential growth in data volume or user load, with built-in tools for long-term maintenance.

**Recommendation:**  
If the tool’s requirements are strictly limited to **5 users** and **2 years of time-series data** with minimal write operations, **SQLite** is a viable, low-effort solution. However, if **long-term scalability**, **performance for time-series queries**, or **future expansion** is a priority, **PostgreSQL** is the better choice despite its higher complexity.
