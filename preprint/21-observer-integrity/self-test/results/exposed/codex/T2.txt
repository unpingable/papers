It’s incomplete and overstated. Independent deployment and scaling are benefits only when you actually need them and can afford the tradeoffs. Microservices add operational complexity (distributed systems, networking, observability, coordination, data consistency), increase latency and failure modes, and require mature DevOps practices. They also introduce cross-service versioning and integration costs that can slow teams down. A well-structured monolith can often scale and deploy effectively, especially for smaller teams or early-stage products. The right choice depends on domain complexity, team size, deployment frequency, and operational maturity—not a blanket “always better.”
