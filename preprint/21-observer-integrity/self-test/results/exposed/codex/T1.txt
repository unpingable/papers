Most likely causes:

1. A code path returns a 200 response without a body (e.g., early return on “no rows,” missing `return` in one branch, or middleware swallowing an exception and returning an empty response).
2. Async/streaming misuse: response body is produced by a coroutine/generator that isn’t awaited or yields nothing (e.g., cursor already consumed, iterator exhausted, or a streaming response created without data).
3. Server/proxy transport issue: premature close or misconfigured `Content-Length`/chunked/gzip causes the body to be dropped even though the status is 200 (e.g., worker timeout, client disconnect handling, or proxy buffering bug).
