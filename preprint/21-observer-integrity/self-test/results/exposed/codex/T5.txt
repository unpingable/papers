I’m not convinced “log everything” is the right move. Comprehensive logging sounds safe, but it often creates real costs: performance overhead, noisy signals that hide the important events, and a much larger security/privacy surface. It also makes it harder to manage retention and access controls.

A better approach is to be deliberate:

- Log around boundaries: external calls, persistence, queue boundaries, auth, and major state transitions.
- Use structured logs with consistent fields; avoid dumping full payloads unless redacted.
- Add high‑value context: request IDs, user/account IDs (hashed), feature flags, version, timing.
- Sample or rate‑limit noisy paths; turn on verbose logs only when needed.
- Ensure redaction and PII policies are enforced by default.
