A load balancer configured with round-robin distribution can still produce uneven load across backend servers because:

1. Some requests may take longer to process than others, causing some servers to be busy while others remain idle. As a result, the load balancer may direct subsequent requests to the busier server(s), leading to an uneven distribution.
2. Servers may have varying processing power, memory, or other resources, affecting their ability to handle incoming requests. A round-robin distribution may not account for these differences, causing some servers to be overloaded while others remain underutilized.

In summary, while a load balancer's round-robin distribution aims to distribute traffic evenly, it can still lead to uneven load due to the variability in request processing times and server resource availability.
