The argument is incomplete because it only focuses on *potential* benefits of microservices (independent deployment and scaling) and ignores the drawbacks. While those benefits are valid, microservices introduce significant complexity in areas like distributed tracing, inter-service communication, and operational overhead. They aren't inherently "better" – the optimal architecture depends on the specific context and trade-offs involved. A monolith might be simpler to develop and deploy initially, and be more performant, depending on the application.
